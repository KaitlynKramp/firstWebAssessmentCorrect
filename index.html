Background on Axolotls

Shrouded in mystery, axolotls are one of the most interesting animals on our planet. These amphibians have become increasingly popular over the years, and have even been added into the popular game Minecraft.Click here to be taken to the Minecraft axolotl wiki page.

The axolotl was named by the Aztec people after Xolotl, their god of fire and lightning, axolotl can also be translated to Water Dog because of Xolotl's dog like head, and also Water Monster since Xolotl was also the god of monsters. In Aztec mythology, Xolotl needed to be sacrificed so the sun could move, to avoid this he transformed into an axolotl to avoid capture. He was eventually tracked down by the wind god and sacrificed. Axolotls were important to the Aztec people both spiritually and as food since they were part of their diet. They also stirred them into a syrup as a folk remedy for respiratory ailments. Today, axolotls are still eaten and are especially popular in Japan.

Axolotls are native only to lake Xochimilco in the valley of Mexico, as well as the canals and waterways of Mexico City. Axolotls are critically endangered in the wild with an estimated 1000 or fewer left. This is due to the invasive fish in their lake and a loss of habitat due to droughts and human interference.
