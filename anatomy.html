Axolotl Anatomy

Although axolotls are a subspecies of salamander, they obtain certain qualities that are very unique. Such as, their rare condition called neoteny which allows them to maintain their juvenile characteristics into adulthood, like their feathery gills and a long dorsal fin. This means that, unlike salamanders, axolotls sustain a fully aquatic lifestyle into adulthood. They are descended from the tiger salamander and have inhabited central Mexico for only about the last 10 000 years. Scientists believe that it’s because of their stable habitat that they were never forced to evolve further and thus retained neoteny. The axolotls most notable ability is how they’re able to regenerate multiple body parts including their limbs, heart, parts of the brain, spinal cord, and lungs. In addition to regeneration, they are also remarkably resistant to cancer, and can also shift their hue a few shades lighter or darker for camouflage. The axolotl is a very interesting animal and has, and will continue being an interesting subject for science.
